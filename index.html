About Me



Hello! My name is Samuel Ayomide Olatunbosun. I was born and raised in Lagos, Nigeria. I am a full-time student at the University of Texas at Austin, studying Electrical and Computer Engineering with a focus on software engineering. Throughout my life, I have faced a great deal of challenges and obstacles that have stood in the way of my success. However, time and time again, I have gotten back up on my two feet with the help of God and my mother.

Whether it ranged from struggling to adapt to the new American school environment after spending a great deal of time in my home country of Nigeria, to being denied from my school of choice, The University of Texas at Austin, during my senior year of high school, to receiving astoundingly low scores on my exams in high school and college, my mother and God have always been the shelter I run to in times of tribulation. They have helped me get back on my two feet by ensuring that everything will be okay and emphasizing that the road towards success is not a straight line, but one with a lot of ups and downs, one just needs the perseverance to get up after those disheartening moments. As Sylvester Stallone said, "It’s not about how hard you hit. It’s about how hard you can get hit and keep moving forward."

This mindset has led me from failing to adapt to the American education system to attending a top 10 engineering school in the country. It is what has led me from being denied by the University of Texas at Austin the first time to being accepted the following year. It is what has led me from receiving astoundingly low scores on my exams to excelling on the subsequent tests thrown my way. And this mindset is what will carry me through the tribulations that are yet to come.
